Europe faces an escalating water quality crisis. The EU Water Framework Directive mandates ecological compliance. Stop pollution before it reaches critical waterways.

The Dual Crisis of Agricultural Runoff

When synthetic fertilizers wash into waterways during heavy rainfall, it triggers a devastating chain reaction impacting both the environment and the economy.

Ecological & Governmental Toll

Runoff causes severe eutrophication, toxic algae blooms, and the collapse of marine ecosystems, creating "dead zones". Governments face massive cleanup costs and looming EU compliance fines.

Impacts: Marine Life, Aquaculture, EU Compliance

The Financial Bleed

Farmers operate on tight margins where fertilizer is a major expense. When nutrients are washed away, invested capital is lost, and crops suffer from under-nutrition, leading to stunted growth.

Impacts: Farmer Margins, Crop Yields

About Runoff Risk Score
The Runoff Risk Score (0-100) is a weighted calculation combining current soil saturation, 48-hour forecasted precipitation, and relative humidity. A higher score indicates a greater probability of agricultural nutrients washing into nearby waterways.
Soil Moisture
-- m³/m³
Volumetric Water Content: Measures soil saturation. Values above 0.50 m³/m³ indicate critical saturation and high runoff risk.
Temperature
-- °C
Air Temperature: Monitored at 2m height. Optimal spraying range is 2°C to 30°C for effective nutrient uptake.
Wind Speed
-- km/h
Wind Velocity: Speeds above 15 km/h significantly increase fertilizer drift risk into nearby ecosystems.
Rain (48h)
-- mm
Total Forecasted Rain: Accumulation over the next 48 hours. Values >15mm combined with saturation drive runoff.
Humidity
-- %
Relative Humidity: Amount of moisture in the air. High humidity (>80%) slows evaporation and prolongs soil saturation.
Runoff Score
-- /100
Runoff Probability: A weighted index (0-100) predicting the likelihood of nutrients entering waterways. Scores above 70 are critical.
